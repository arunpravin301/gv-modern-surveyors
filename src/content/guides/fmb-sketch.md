When applying for a home loan, one of the first documents your bank will ask for is the Field Measurement Book (FMB) sketch. But what exactly is it, and why is it so critical?

## The Blueprint of Your Land
An FMB sketch is an official record maintained by the Tahsildar office. It outlines the exact geometric dimensions, boundaries, and adjacent properties of your specific survey number. 

Without an FMB, a piece of land is just an abstract concept. The FMB provides the mathematical proof of the land's existence and shape.

## Why Banks Demand It
Banks need absolute certainty that the property they are financing:
1. Actually exists in the exact dimensions claimed.
2. Is not encroaching on government or adjacent private land.
3. Matches the details in the Sale Deed and Patta.

A surveyor's job is to take the official FMB sketch and physically mark those exact coordinates on the ground using a Total Station. This proves to the bank that the physical reality matches the government record.
